The Real Struggle

The Problem: Statistics Was a Nightmare

Alex, an adult learner with learning disabilities, was going back to college to finish his degree. Statistics was required—and it was a nightmare.

The Learning Gap

Traditional tutoring was expensive ($30-50/hr), time-limited, and didn't click. Generic AI assistants gave cookie-cutter answers disconnected from his real life as a brewer at **Ore Dock Brewing Company in Marquette, Michigan**.

He needed 24/7 help but couldn't afford ongoing tutoring. Learning disabilities made traditional "one-size-fits-all" approaches ineffective.

What Didn't Work

  • **Generic AI:** Cookie-cutter answers, abstract concepts.
  • **Textbooks:** Examples felt meaningless and disconnected.
  • **Traditional Tutors:** Expensive, time-limited, and didn't adapt to his learning style.
  • **One-Size-Fits-All:** Ineffective for his specific learning disabilities.
The Human Touch

The Turning Point: One Hour of Listening

Alex reached out with a simple question: "Can you build me an AI tutor that actually gets how I learn?"

No pitch. No templates. Just **one hour on the phone**, talking. That's where the magic happened. We didn't automate understanding—we focused on it.

We talked about:

  • How Alex learns best (real-world comparisons, not abstract theory)
  • His work as a brewer at Ore Dock Brewing Company
  • His life in Marquette, Michigan
  • His sense of humor and communication style
  • How his learning disabilities affect traditional study methods

Then we built **Molly**—a custom AI statistics tutor trained specifically on Alex's learning style, industry background, and the way his brain works.

The Custom Solution

Meet Molly: The Custom AI Tutor

Molly isn't ChatGPT. She's a custom-trained Gemini Gem built for **one person: Alex**. She teaches statistics **the way Alex's brain works**.

Brewery-Themed Explanations

When Alex asks about probability, Molly explains it using batch fermentation data. Hypothesis testing? Quality control scenarios from the brewery floor. Statistics finally makes sense through his real-world context.

Built for Alex's Learning Style

Molly knows Alex needs real-world context first, then theory. She knows his sense of humor. She adapts to his learning disabilities, breaking down complex concepts into digestible pieces until they click.

24/7 Availability & Zero Judgment

No scheduling. No waiting. No anxiety around asking "dumb questions." Alex studies when it works for him, and Molly patiently repeats explanations in different ways until he understands.
